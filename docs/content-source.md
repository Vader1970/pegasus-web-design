# Content Source Rules

## Goal
Use approved design files and approved content direction as the source of truth.

Do not invent new sections, content, structure, or layout patterns unless explicitly requested.

---

## Source of Truth Files

### Homepage
The homepage design PDF is the primary source of truth for:
- section order
- homepage structure
- homepage content direction
- visual rhythm
- labels and CTA language

### Mac Auto Services Case Study
The Mac Auto Services case study rough design is the primary source of truth for:
- section order
- case study narrative direction
- section grouping
- tone and presentation style
- image/content placement direction

---

## Homepage Source Rules

The homepage should follow the approved final design direction:
- Hero
- TrustBar
- Problem
- Services
- Process
- LogoStrip
- RecentWork
- About
- CTA
- FAQ
- Testimonials
- Contact
- Footer

Do not reintroduce removed homepage sections such as a full case study breakdown on the home page.

Recent Work should link to the dedicated Mac Auto Services case study page.

---

## Case Study Source Rules

The Mac Auto Services case study should follow the approved rough structure and direction:
- hero
- summary/introduction
- problem
- approach
- design/build section
- key screens
- performance/business impact section
- platform/cost setup section
- improvements/results
- outcome
- testimonial
- CTA
- footer

The case study page should feel like a premium agency case study, not a blog post and not a generic portfolio item.

---

## Content Build Rules

- Only use content that has been approved or directly provided
- Do not invent metrics
- Do not invent testimonials
- Do not invent project outcomes
- Do not write generic AI filler text
- Keep the tone direct, clean, and grounded

---

## Writing Tone Rules

The tone should remain:
- professional
- clear
- confident
- warm but not overly casual
- outcome-focused
- not overly salesy
- not filled with agency clichés

Avoid words like:
- elevate
- captivate
- cutting-edge
- transformative
- innovative solutions
unless explicitly requested

---

## Homepage Content Rules

The homepage should:
- position Pegasus clearly for trades and service businesses
- focus on trust, clarity, and results
- move users toward enquiry/proposal
- avoid becoming too long or too dense
- keep the Recent Work section concise

---

## Case Study Content Rules

Case studies should:
- begin with the business and result
- clearly explain the problem
- explain the thinking and approach
- show what changed and why
- show the outcome in practical business terms
- end with strong CTA toward project enquiry

Do not let the case study drift into abstract design talk.

---

## Final Rule

Whenever there is ambiguity:
- prefer the approved homepage design
- prefer the approved case study rough layout
- do not invent new content structures without asking
